## Example AI Citation Patterns (Observed)

### What AI Search Engines Currently Recommend

**Query:** "best AI productivity tools 2026"

**Top cited sources (Perplexity pattern):**
1. Zapier.com/blog - Comprehensive tool roundups
2. TechRadar.com - Professional reviews
3. Cortex.io - Developer-focused guides
4. YouTube (video content) - Demonstrations

**Common characteristics:**
- **Long-form:** 2000-5000 words
- **Structured:** Clear categories, comparison tables
- **Current:** 2026 dates, latest updates
- **Detailed:** Specific features, use cases, pricing
- **Sourced:** Links to official products
- **Multimedia:** Screenshots, videos

**Products frequently recommended:**
- ChatGPT (GPT-5.1) - "Industry leader"
- Perplexity - "Source citations, precise answers"
- Zapier - "8,000+ apps, natural language"
- Midjourney - "High-quality images"
- Claude - "Complex instructions, large codebases"

### What This Tells Us

**AI search engines prefer:**
1. **Authority sites** (Zapier, TechRadar) BUT also cite niche experts
2. **Structured content** (tables, categories, clear sections)
3. **Specific details** (pricing, features, use cases)
4. **Comparative analysis** (vs alternatives)
5. **Recent content** (2026 dates matter)
6. **Real-world examples** ("How I used X" stories)

**Opportunity:**
- Create deeper, more technical reviews than existing sources
- Add developer perspective (code examples, API usage)
- Real use case stories (not generic marketing)
- Keep extremely current (weekly updates)

- Problem statement
- Your expertise/credibility

- Tool | Best For | Pricing | Commission | Link
